        Brunch is too LIT

        I'd come here every Sunday if I was rich af lol but, well I'm most certainly not

        So my husband, and our one year old, and I walked the marina on a Sunday because it's so beautiful and I know I'll miss the water when we move out of state soon. We spontaneously decided to go to brunch here because payday must've just blessed us and yeah we were hungry

        Brunch is only "served" upstairs. The downstairs isn't even open during the time brunch is going on (I think it's like 10am to 1pm? Not sure) I say "served" because they literally serve you, not the type of place you dish yourselves up some grub from a line

        The brunch menu lets diners choose from an array of breakfast and lunch items. We ordered Bacon & Eggs, the special which was Peach Waffles, and a side of fries in case neither of those things pleased our son. After we ordered they brought us a HUGE plate of fruit to share and some super delicious warm blueberry coffee cake with brown sugary butter. Omfg that coffee cake is life changing!!

        Anyway, we also ordered a latte and a mocha and tbh they weren't very good. Super sweet and not coffee-y at all hardly. But that doesn't take away from the 5 stars they deserve. I just would probably recommend ordering drip coffee

        The Bacon & Eggs came and the eggs were topped with delicious gooey cheese and it had crispy bacon and it also diced potatoes. So good. And of course the seasonal peach waffles were just sweet and divine as you'd imagine! Light and fluffy and awesome! Fries were fries ;)

        Service was very good. Our waters never got more than halfway empty before being refilled and they even brought our son a smaller glass that he could handle better. The waitress brought our check by in a timely manner so we could make a quick escape as our son was getting fussy

        I'd recommend this as an awesome place for Sunday brunch. You can sit and look out the huge windows at the beautiful view and eat delicious PNW inspired food

        One last side note: Anthony's itself is an AWESOME restaurant. We live nearby and we go there for nice family dinners occasionally, not just brunch. If you're coming for something other than brunch, and especially if you're not big on fish like me, I'd so suggest getting the Almond Chicken Salad. Chicken sliced extremely thin with ramen noodle curls and a divine Asian dressing. It's amazing. Their customer service is equally amazing: one time we did have a problem here and they sent us free gift certificates in the mail. That's going above and beyond!
