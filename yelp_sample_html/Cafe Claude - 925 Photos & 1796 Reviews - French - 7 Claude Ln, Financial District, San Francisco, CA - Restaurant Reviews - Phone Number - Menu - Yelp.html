      My friend has been raving about Cafe Claude for months now so I've been wanting to try it for awhile. We finally made plans to eat here on Saturday night for my belated birthday dinner and it was well worth the wait. Cafe Claude looks like it belongs a few blocks away on Belden Place, with the rest of the French bistro litter. However, this cafe has staked claims on its own alley, and rightfully so. The food, service, and live jazz were amazing! It's not easy to find all three in good quality under one roof. We had the Truffle Mousse pate, a cheese plate, Tuna Tartare, and some of the best pork loins ever (served with caramelized apples in this divine sauce). I also had this yummy drink called Le Petite Ecolier - pineapple juice, cranberry juice, grenadine, and Sprite. It was perfect. The prices are very reasonable too - about $10-12 for small plates and $18-24 for main entrees. It was also nice that they didn't rush us out of the restaurant even though there were people waiting to be seated. This is a lovely little restaurant - I am definitely looking forward to going back.
